Year 1 Block C

🧠 Deep Learning

Entering the world of neural networks and deep learning, building image classification models while exploring the critical topics of responsible AI, explainability, and human-centered design in AI systems.

🔥 PyTorch 🖼️ CNNs 🔍 Explainable AI 👤 Human-Centered AI

Project Overview

This project marked my transition from classical machine learning to deep learning. I learned to build and train neural networks from scratch, understanding the architecture of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and their application to image classification tasks.

Beyond technical implementation, this block emphasized the ethical dimensions of AI—how to build systems that are transparent, fair, and aligned with human values.

Key Components

  • Introduction to Deep Learning: Neural network fundamentals, backpropagation, activation functions, and the training process.
  • Responsible & Explainable AI: Techniques for making model decisions interpretable, including SHAP values, attention visualization, and saliency maps.
  • Human-Centered AI: Designing AI systems that augment human capabilities while respecting user autonomy and agency.
  • Image Classification: Building CNN architectures for visual recognition tasks, from simple classifiers to transfer learning approaches.

Technical Deep Dive

Working with PyTorch, I implemented various neural network architectures:

  • Feedforward networks with multiple hidden layers
  • Convolutional Neural Networks (LeNet, VGG-style)
  • Transfer learning with pre-trained models (ResNet, VGG)
  • Data augmentation and regularization techniques
  • Learning rate scheduling and optimization strategies

Responsible AI Focus

A unique aspect of this project was the emphasis on AI ethics and responsibility:

  • Analyzed model biases and their sources in training data
  • Implemented explainability techniques to understand model decisions
  • Explored fairness metrics and mitigation strategies
  • Documented model limitations and appropriate use cases
  • Considered privacy implications of image-based AI systems

Learning Outcomes

  • Design and train deep neural networks for image classification
  • Apply explainability techniques to interpret model predictions
  • Evaluate AI systems through an ethical and human-centered lens
  • Identify and mitigate biases in machine learning systems
